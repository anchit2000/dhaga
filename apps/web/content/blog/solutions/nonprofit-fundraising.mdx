## Stewardship is a long game, and memory is the bottleneck

Ask a seasoned development officer why a major gift came in and you'll rarely
hear "the appeal letter." You'll hear about the six years of coffees, the board
member who made the introduction, the donor's daughter who went through the
program, the campus visit that finally made it real. Major-gift fundraising is
relationship work stretched across years, and the relationship is the whole
asset.

Your donor database is built to be the system of record: gift history, pledges,
soft credits, campaign attribution. That's essential, and Dhaga doesn't try to
be it. What the database is *not* built to hold is the human texture â that a
donor cares about first-generation students because they were one, that they
hate being asked over the phone, that their spouse is the real decision-maker
above a certain size. That context lives in your memory, and memory fades
between visits that are months apart.

Dhaga's job is to hold that texture for you. Capture what you learn and it
becomes a **private knowledge graph** â structured people, the organizations and
committees they sit on, and the relationships between them. Every fact it derives
keeps a receipt back to the note it came from, so "prefers to give
anonymously" is always traceable to the visit where you heard it, not a hunch you
half-remember two campaigns later.

## Capture the visit without breaking the moment

You learn the most where taking notes would be rude â across the table at a
cultivation lunch, walking a donor through the new wing, in the receiving line at
a gala. So capture has to happen after, in seconds, or the detail is gone.

- **Scan a badge or business card** at the gala or the board dinner and get a
  structured contact, not a stack of cards in a drawer.
- **Voice-note the debrief** in the car afterward: "Visited Eleanor, she lit up
  talking about the youth arts program, her granddaughter just started violin,
  wants to do something meaningful before year-end." Dhaga extracts the people,
  the interests, and the relationships.
- **Web quick-add or the browser extension** to capture a prospect you're
  researching between meetings.
- **Import** your existing contacts from a CSV or a LinkedIn export so years of
  relationships seed the graph on day one.

The point isn't data entry for its own sake. It's that the moment you learn what
a donor truly cares about, it lands somewhere you can query instead of
evaporating before the next portfolio review.
