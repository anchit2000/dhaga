<Callout title="The short version">
In real estate, the deals you lose are rarely the ones you fought for and
missed — they're the ones you forgot to follow up on. A lead that went quiet, a
past client whose closing anniversary slipped by, a farm neighbourhood you meant
to keep warm. Dhaga is a private relationship memory for your whole sphere of
influence: capture every conversation in seconds, ask it plain-English questions
like "who haven't I spoken to in six months?", and let it quietly remind you of
the details that turn a contact into a referral. It's your memory, not a lead
marketplace — your data stays yours.

Ask any experienced agent where their business comes from and you'll hear the
same answer: past clients and referrals. The database *is* the business. And yet
most agents run that database out of their head, a stack of business cards, and a
phone full of names with no context. The lead you met at an open house three
weeks ago is now just a number you're afraid to call because you can't remember
what they were looking for.

Dhaga doesn't try to be a transaction manager or a lead-gen machine. It's the
part that's actually hard: remembering people, and remembering *why they matter*,
across hundreds of relationships and years of time.

## Capture the conversation, not just the contact

A name and a phone number is not a relationship. The relationship is in the
details — they're relocating for a job in the spring, they hated the third
bedroom, the wife wants a bigger yard, they're pre-approved but nervous about
rates.

Dhaga is built to capture that with almost no friction, wherever you are:

- **Scan a card or an open-house sign-in** — a photo becomes a structured
  contact, so you're not typing at a stoplight.
- **Talk instead of type.** Walk out of a showing and leave a voice note — "met
  the Hendersons, second baby due in June, want to be in the school district
  before then, top of budget is around 650" — and Dhaga turns spoken words into
  structured facts attached to that contact.
- **Quick-add from the web or the browser extension** when a lead comes in
  online.
- **Import what you already have** from a CSV or a LinkedIn export, so your
  existing sphere comes with you on day one.

The point is that capture takes seconds, in the moment, so the detail is still
fresh — instead of a note you'll write "later" and never do.

## Never let a lead go cold in silence

The most expensive words in real estate are "I'll circle back with them next
week." Leads don't announce that they've gone cold; they just quietly stop being
top of mind.

Because every note becomes part of a private, queryable graph, you can simply
ask Dhaga in plain English:

- "Which leads haven't I followed up with in the last two weeks?"
- "Who was looking in Riverside under 500k?"
- "Show me everyone I met at an open house this month."

The answers come only from your own notes — and when Dhaga doesn't know
something, it tells you rather than making it up. When it's time to reach back
out, Dhaga can help you draft the follow-up, grounded in what you actually
discussed, so it reads like a person who remembers them, not a template.

## Turn past clients into referrals and reviews

A closing is not the end of a relationship — it's the start of the most valuable
one you have. Past clients refer, and they leave the reviews that win the next
listing. But that only happens if you stay in their life without being a pest.

Dhaga helps you keep those threads alive:

| The moment | What Dhaga remembers | Why it earns business |
|---|---|---|
| Closing / move-in anniversary | The date, and the property | A one-year "how's the house?" note that feels personal, not automated |
| A great closing | That the client was thrilled | The right time to gently ask for a review or referral |
| Life events you were told about | New baby, renovation, job change | A reason to reach out that isn't "do you know anyone selling?" |
| A referral they sent you | Who referred whom | So you actually thank the source and close the loop |

You choose what to keep track of. The proactive, opt-in watchlist surfaces the
people and moments you care about — they don't broadcast your book of business
anywhere or watch anyone behind your back.

## Keep a geographic farm warm

Farming a neighbourhood is a long game of consistency, and consistency is
exactly what falls apart when you're busy. Dhaga treats your farm the way it
treats any other part of your sphere: as people you can slice and recall.

Ask "who do I know on Maple Street?" or "which contacts are in the Oakridge
subdivision?" and you get the list, with the context attached — who's a
homeowner you've talked to, who mentioned they might sell in a couple of years,
who you met at the block party. When you send the quarterly market update, you
know who's actually in the farm and what you last spoke about, so a mailing turns
into a real conversation.

## Remember the details that make you human

This is where relationship intelligence quietly wins listings. Agents don't lose
to a better price nearly as often as they lose to someone who felt more
*attentive*.

Dhaga remembers the small things because you told it once:

- The kids' names and ages — and that they'll be changing schools.
- That they said "we'd want a bigger yard in about two years" — a two-year-out
  seller you'd otherwise forget.
- The renovation they were planning, so you can ask how it turned out.
- The dog, the commute they hated, the reason they moved last time.

Every one of these facts keeps a **receipt** — a link back to the exact note it
came from — so you can trust where a detail came from and see the conversation
behind it. Before you walk into a listing appointment or a coffee, Dhaga can pull
together a short brief on that person from your own history, so you arrive
sounding like someone who genuinely remembers them.

## Your sphere is your asset — and it stays yours

Your database of relationships is the single most valuable thing you own as an
agent. Dhaga is built on that principle. It's privacy-first and self-hostable,
you can bring your own API key, and you can export all of your data at any time.
It doesn't sell your contacts, mine them, or make them anyone else's. The
proactive features are opt-in and bounded — a tool that works for you, not a
surveillance service pointed at your clients.
