Everything you know about a person lives on their profile at
**`/app/people/[id]`** — their jobs, contact methods, location, relationships,
and, below that, their **notes and facts**.

![The top of a person's profile showing their jobs, email, location, and relationships](/docs/guide/person-detail.png)

## Add a note

In the **Notes** box on a person's page, either type what you remember or tap
the mic and just talk — voice notes are transcribed for you. The box hints as
much: *"Type — or tap the mic and just talk. Facts get extracted
automatically."*

![A person's profile showing the Facts section with two extracted facts, the Enrich from public web button, and the Notes box with Add note and Voice note buttons](/docs/guide/person-facts-enrich.png)

## Facts get extracted automatically

When you save a note *and cloud AI is enabled on your plan*, Dhaga extracts
structured **facts** from it — for example a personal detail or an intent
("wants an intro to a designer"). Each fact is tagged by kind (**PERSONAL**,
**INTENT**, and so on).

Every extracted fact carries a **receipt**: the source note it came from, shown
as *"FROM NOTE, 23/7/2026"*. This is a core principle — no AI-derived fact
exists without a traceable source you can open and verify.

## Add facts and follow-ups by hand

You don't need cloud AI to build a person's profile. On the free tier — or any
time you'd rather just type it — use **＋ Add fact** in the Facts section and
**＋ Add follow-up** in the Follow-ups section. Manual facts have no source note
(nothing to trace back to) and are saved as already-verified, since you entered
them yourself. Everything else in Dhaga — contacts, jobs, contact methods,
relationships, groups — is fully editable by hand too, so the app is completely
usable with no AI at all.

<Callout title="This is the user's data about third parties">
Facts are extracted from *your* notes about the people you meet. Nothing is
collected silently — a fact only appears because you wrote the note it came
from.
</Callout>

## Editing, deleting, and the cascade

- **Edit or delete a fact** directly if the extraction got something wrong or
  the detail no longer applies.
- **Delete the source note** and every fact derived from it is tombstoned along
  with it — the receipt guarantees Dhaga knows exactly which facts to remove.
- **Delete the person** and the cascade runs all the way down: notes, facts,
  graph edges, and embeddings all go with them.

Want to add outside context to a person? See
[Enrich from the web](/docs/guide/enrich-from-web).
