**`/app/settings`** is where you tune how Dhaga behaves and connect it to the
rest of your tools.

![The top of the Dhaga settings page showing billing, security, calendar, suggestions, card-photo, voice, API-key, and import sections](/docs/guide/settings.png)

## What you can configure

- **Billing** — your plan and usage.
- **Security** — two-factor authentication (2FA) for your account.
- **Calendar** — connect Google Calendar so Dhaga can work against your
  schedule.
- **Suggestions** — the daily reach-out suggestions and digest that feed your
  Home briefing.
- **Card photos** — whether the original card image is kept after a scan.
- **Voice input** — which speech-to-text engine transcribes your voice notes.
- **API keys** — personal access tokens for the API and the browser extension,
  and for scripted [exports](/docs/self-hosting/deploy#backups--migration).

<Callout title="Some sections depend on configuration">
Billing only appears when the instance has Stripe configured, and calendar/
email features need their providers wired up. On a plain self-host several of
these sections are intentionally absent — see [Self-hosting](/docs/self-hosting).
</Callout>

## Import your contacts

The **Import contacts** section brings an existing address book into Dhaga from
a **Google** or **LinkedIn CSV** export. The file is **parsed in your browser**
— it doesn't get uploaded somewhere to be processed — and the parsed people land
in your graph ready to enrich with notes.
